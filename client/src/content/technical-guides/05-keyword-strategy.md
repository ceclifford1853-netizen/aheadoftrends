# AEO Keyword Strategy: Entity-Based Research for the AI Citation Economy

The death of traditional keyword strategy is perhaps the most liberating shift in the AEO paradigm. For decades, marketers enslaved themselves to keyword density metrics, exact-match domains, and semantic approximations—all predicated on the assumption that machines could not truly understand meaning. Today, LLMs understand meaning with remarkable sophistication, and our research methodologies must evolve accordingly.

The atom of AEO keyword strategy is not the keyword—it is the entity. Rather than optimizing for search volume and competition scores, AEO keyword research identifies the entities, concepts, and relationships that AI engines recognize as authoritative within specific knowledge domains. This methodology produces more durable visibility, more qualified traffic, and measurably higher citation probability.

## The Fundamental Shift: From Keywords to Entities

Traditional keyword research answered a single question: "What are people searching for?" The methodology was straightforward—compile seed keywords, expand through keyword tools, analyze search volume and competition, prioritize opportunities with favorable volume-to-competition ratios.

Entity-based research answers a more sophisticated question: "What does the AI knowledge graph recognize as authoritative within this domain, and how can our entity become central to that representation?"

This distinction matters enormously. Keyword rankings fluctuate with algorithmic changes, competitive pressure, and SERP feature modifications. Entity authority, once established, is extraordinarily durable. You are not fighting for a position on a page—you are building infrastructure within the AI's understanding of your market.

### Why Keywords Die and Entities Persist

Consider a hypothetical scenario: You optimize for the keyword "enterprise workflow automation Southeast Asia" and achieve position one. A competitor launches a better-funded SEO campaign. You lose the position. Your visibility collapses.

Alternatively, you build entity authority around "Enterprise Workflow Automation" as a domain concept. Your brand becomes recognized as a primary entity within that concept's knowledge graph representation. A competitor cannot dislodge your entity presence through SEO tactics alone—they must match your broader infrastructure investment across citations, relationships, and authoritative content.

## The Entity Research Methodology

AEO keyword research proceeds through four phases, each building upon the previous.

### Phase 1: Domain Entity Mapping

Begin by mapping the core entities within your domain. For each entity, identify:
- **Type**: What Schema.org type best represents this entity?
- **Properties**: What defining characteristics does this entity possess?
- **Relationships**: What other entities does this entity connect to?
- **Regional Variants**: How does this entity manifest across target markets?

For a B2B SaaS company selling enterprise workflow automation in Southeast Asia, core entities might include:
- `Workflow Automation` (Concept/SoftwareApplication)
- `Southeast Asia` (Place with economic sub-regions)
- `Enterprise Software` (Product category)
- `Digital Transformation` (Concept)
- `Manufacturing Sector` (Industry vertical)

### Phase 2: Knowledge Graph Analysis

For each core entity, analyze how AI engines currently represent it. This requires:
- **LLM Query Testing**: Ask multiple AI engines questions centered on each entity
- **Citation Tracking**: Identify which sources are currently cited for entity-related queries
- **Relationship Mapping**: Observe which entities co-occur in authoritative answers
- **Gap Identification**: Find underserved sub-domains where your entity could become primary

The goal is to understand the current knowledge graph structure—not to accept it, but to identify where you can establish commanding presence.

### Phase 3: Semantic Neighborhood Expansion

Entities do not exist in isolation. They exist within semantic neighborhoods—clusters of related concepts that co-occur in authoritative discourse. Expanding your semantic neighborhood requires:

**Adjacency Claims**: Declare relationships to neighboring entities through structured data and content references. If your workflow automation platform serves the manufacturing sector, explicitly establish that relationship through Organization schema's `areaServed` and content references to manufacturing-specific use cases.

**Terminology Calibration**: Match the exact terminology that AI engines recognize for your entities. "Enterprise workflow automation" and "business process management software" may describe similar concepts, but AI knowledge graphs weight them differently. Use tools and direct testing to identify the highest-authority terminology.

**Question Pattern Mapping**: Identify the questions AI engines answer about your entities. These question patterns become the framework for your FAQ and HowTo content.

### Phase 4: Entity Velocity Planning

Entity authority requires ongoing demonstration. Static presence decays as the AI web evolves. Your research should produce a velocity plan:

- **Publication Cadence**: How often will you publish new authoritative content referencing your core entities?
- **Citation Strategy**: Which third-party publications will you pursue for entity corroboration?
- **Relationship Expansion**: Which new entity relationships will you establish each quarter?
- **Regional Rollout**: In what order will you expand entity presence across target markets?

## Practical Implementation

Translating entity research into content strategy follows a predictable pattern:

### Core Entity Content

Each core entity deserves at least one comprehensive pillar page that:
- Defines the entity with exhaustive thoroughness
- Establishes entity relationships through structured links and schema
- Demonstrates authority through original research, data, or methodology
- Targets primary question patterns the entity answers

### Semantic Cluster Content

Around each pillar, deploy supporting content that:
- Explores entity sub-types and specializations
- Addresses specific use cases and applications
- Answers detailed question patterns
- Links back to the core entity pillar

### FAQ Expansion

Entity-adjacent FAQ content has exceptional citation probability. Every core entity and major sub-entity should generate 10-20 FAQ questions with comprehensive answers and proper FAQPage schema.

## Measuring Entity Strategy Success

Traditional SEO metrics—rankings, traffic, backlinks—remain partially relevant but should be supplemented with entity-specific KPIs:

- **Entity Citation Rate**: How often does your brand appear in AI-generated answers?
- **Entity Relationship Count**: How many verified entity relationships does your brand possess?
- **Semantic Neighborhood Share**: What percentage of authoritative discourse in your domain mentions your entity?
- **Citation Position**: Are you the primary cited source or secondary reference?
